Cucumber

A Minimalist Commenting System

So, here is "Cucumber" — my very own commenting system. Still in the works... As of today (20 May, 2015), users can login via Facebook, Google, or Github, and leave comments, but can't remove comments yet. It's open source, so you can feel free to edit code in the css and js and whatever. I'm using Firebase to store my data. A little bit of a learning curve, but I plan to post a tutorial to teach you how to set up your own cucumber-firebase commenting system. Eventually, I would love to add inline commenting features, as well as image / video posting capabilities.

I'm calling this "Cucumber" because I though of calling it "Qcomber" at first, because it was like "quick comments"...  But I thought that was lame. So, "Cucumber" it is!

I think Disqus is cool, but I just feel like it's a lot of clutter. And I don't like the idea of signing in through Disqus. Why can't I just sign in through the usual social media??? Speaking of which, the procedure of adding other social media (like Twitter) to sign in is pretty similar to the rest.

So, drop me a line or a comment or whatever...

Tutorial

To use cucumber, you will first need (1) a Firebase account, and (2) Facebook / Google / Github apps. Then, all you need to do is reference the relevant javascript and css files. I will walk you through the set-up of the firebase account and google app, and direct you to a github repository for a simple demo. The rest should be quite easy to figure out. But feel free to leave me a comment, if you so desire.

Firebase Account

If you don't already have one, you should first set up a firebase account. Firebase is a realtime database that allows users to easily authenticate through Facebook, Google, Github, Twitter, or email and password.

Firebase App

Next, you should create a firebase app. That sounds really complicated, right? But it's really just naming things. It's like setting up an email account. Just go to your firebase account and look for this page:

Then in the "CREATE NEW APP" box under App Names, type a name for your app. (e.g. 'Cucumber'). You don't need to bother filling out the URL, it does it automatically assuming you do it right the first time.

Login & Authentication

Next, go to the "Login & Authentication" tab. To add cucumber to your site, you will need to enter your website's URL. Just the homepage will do. And at the bottom of the page, go through each of the tabs and enable the services you want. The screen below is what your page should look like for enabling Google authentication.

Google App

Last of all, to fill out the Google Client ID and Secret, you simply click on 'Learn more' and follow the instructions under 'Configuring Your Application. The configuration should take no more than 5 minutes. It's a short section, so don't skip anything. Skipping may cause you grief.

And that's it for the apps. Now, all you need to do, is put the right chunks of code in the right places. So, just look at this demo on my Github.
